# Copyright, public-domain assessment & takedown

## How we decide a text is public domain

A work is published here only if it passes an automated check applying **all** of these rules:

- **Life + 70** — every author died at least 70 full calendar years ago.
- **US 95-year rule** — the work was first published at least 95 years ago. (Both rules must
  pass: a work can be public domain in one country but not another, so we require both.)
- **Edition rights** — we transcribe from an original or early edition, excluding any modern
  critical apparatus, commentary, or new typesetting that could carry its own copyright.
- **Translation source** — our translations are made only from our own transcription of the
  public-domain original, never from an existing modern translation.

The facts behind each decision (death dates, first-publication date, edition) are **sourced and
recorded** with every work, and the assessment is recomputed automatically on every change.

> This is a good-faith engineering process, **not legal advice**. Public-domain status varies by
> country; we aim to be conservative.

## Licensing of our content

Our transcriptions, translations, and metadata are dedicated to the public domain under
**CC0 1.0**. The site code is MIT-licensed.

## Takedown & complaints

If you believe a work here is **not** in the public domain (for example, an edition still under
protection, a late first publication, or moral/personality rights), please contact us — see
**[Contact](/contact/)**. On a credible claim we will **unpublish the work promptly** while we
reassess, and we log the outcome. Because the corpus is version-controlled, removal is immediate
and, where required, purged from history.
